The Scorching of Eden

The cause of all this

In general, "scorching" is a warfare technique whereby canisters are dropped from above onto a landscape which then release inflammable gasses and spray napalm fluid in a large radius, before exploding, igniting these substances and sending large pieces of shrapnel, sometimes capable of shattering walls, in a multitude of directions. It has not been used by any faction within the Resemblace.

In [YEAR], the city-state of Eden was attacked and scorched by the city-state of Good Saimon, who had been greatly in disagreement with Eden's expansionist policies in the region. The Saimonites however had vastly underestimated Eden's automated response and were wiped out in the conflict; Eden too was destroyed, but the Knights took swift control and began to build a New Eden.
